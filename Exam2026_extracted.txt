1 Data description
The dataset provided for analysis pertains to a leveling program implemented by a public Chilean uni-
versity. This program offers tutoring to students upon their entry into the university, with a primary focus
on those admitted through inclusive access (denoted as V AI in the dataset).
Students participating in the tutoring program are referred to asBAP students. In the dataset, a student
is classified as a BAP student if BAP = 1 and as a non-BAP student if BAP = 0.
The tutoring program operates on a semester basis, with each semester lasting 16 weeks. At the end
of the first semester, students may choose to continue in the program. If spots become available, new
students can join.
The dataset records the number of tutorials attended by each student. According to the Official Lev-
eling Program guidelines, a student is considered actively engaged if they attend at least 10 tutorials per
semester. Attendance of fewer than 10 tutorials indicates minimal intervention.
Two key academic outcomes are examined:
1. The average grade attained at the end of each semester.
2. The course approval rate, defined as the ratio of approved courses to total enrolled courses.
In Chile’s higher education system, university admissions are determined based on standardized Na-
tional Language and Math test scores, as well as the percentile ranking of students’ secondary school
grades.
The dataset comprises the following variables:
• PTJE_RANKING
Percentile rank of grades obtained in secondary school. Scores above 750 are considered high.

• PTJE_LYC
Language score from the national test, with a national mean of 550 and a standard deviation of 50.
• PTJE_MAT
Mathematics score from the national test, with a national mean of 550 and a standard deviation of
50.
• VAI
Binary variable: V AI = 1 indicates that a student was admitted to the university through inclusive
access. These students are highly likely to have low Mathematics and Language scores. V AI =
0 indicates admission through the national selection system, which is based on Language and
Mathematics scores and the percentile rank of secondary school grades.
• BAP
Binary variable: BAP = 1 indicates that a student participated in the tutoring program during
the first semester; BAP = 0 indicates non-participation. In the second semester, a student is
considered to have participated in the tutoring program if they attended at least one session. If the
corresponding variable is missing, the student did not participate in the second semester.
• Tut_20161
Number of tutoring sessions attended in the first semester. A missing value indicates non-participation.
• Tut_20162
Number of tutoring sessions attended in the second semester. A missing value indicates non-
participation.
• Tasa_aprob_20161
Proportion of approved courses at the end of the first semester.
• Tasa_aprob_20162
Proportion of approved courses at the end of the second semester.
• Prom__20161
Average grade at the end of the first semester (scale from 1 to 7, with 4 as the minimum passing
grade).
• Prom__20162
Average grade at the end of the second semester (scale from 1 to 7, with 4 as the minimum passing
grade).

2 Research questions
Using the partial identification techniques covered in the lectures, let’s address the following questions:
1. Define Zk = 1 if a student attended at least k tutoring sessions during the first semester, and
Zk = 0 if they attended fewer than k sessions. Zk represents the intensity of the treatment. For
BAP students, we aim to identify the threshold value ofk at which tutoring has the greatest impact
on both the average grade and the proportion of approved courses at the end of the first semester.
2. At the start of the second semester, students can choose whether to continue in the tutoring program
or discontinue participation. We will assess which decision leads to the best outcome: remaining
in the program or opting out. For this question, we will consider both BAP students and V AI
students in the second semester.
